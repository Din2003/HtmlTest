Overview

SUMPA: Ang Lihim ng Class President is a psychological horror stage production that explores guilt, buried truth, and manipulation hidden behind leadership and reputation.

This play challenges the audience to question: What happens when silence becomes a crime? Through intense performances and atmospheric staging, the story reveals how one tragic event connects a group of former classmates years later.

Without revealing major plot twists, the play promises suspense, emotional intensity, and a chilling reflection on accountability. It is not just a horror story—it is a story about choice.

The Plot

Ten years after a tragic incident involving a classmate named Eno, a group of former students reunite for what seems to be a harmless thrill.

A game.

A ritual.

A name spelled out in the dark.

When the past resurfaces, hidden guilt begins to crack friendships apart. As fear grows inside an abandoned house, accusations start to rise.

But the biggest secret has yet to be revealed.

Who truly started it all?

          • Jealousy and Insecurity.

The play draws inspiration from real-life social dynamics in school environments—bullying, peer pressure, leadership politics, and hidden jealousy — combined with supernatural horror elements.

           • Young Adults.

           • Audiences interested in psychological and moral-based horror stories.

REFLECTION:

  • HOW DIGITAL MEDIA HELPED PROMOTE THE PLAY

Digital platforms allowed us to reach a wider audience efficiently. Through social media teasers, trailers, and our WordPress promotional website, we were able to generate anticipation before the actual performance.

  • WHAT DESIGN PRINCIPLES THEY APPLIED

We applied:

• Visual hierarchy (clear headings, structured sections)

• Contrast (dark theme aligned with horror genre)

• Consistency (uniform typography and layout)

• Balance (text and multimedia integration)

  • HOW THEY ENSURED ETHICAL MEDIA USE

All photos, videos, and music used were either:

• Original student creations

• Properly credited

• Free-to-use resources with acknowledgment We ensured no copyrighted materials were used without permission.

  • CHALLENGES ENCOUNTERED

• Coordinating multimedia production schedules

• Editing trailer footage

• Maintaining consistency in website design

• Balancing creativity with technical functionality

We learned that storytelling does not end on stage—it continues through digital platforms. Website presentation greatly influences audience perception.
